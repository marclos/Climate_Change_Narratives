Impacts

  • In the coming decades, Louisiana will become warmer, and both floods and droughts may become more severe.

  • Unlike most of the nation, Louisiana did not become warmer during the last century.

  • But soils have become drier, annual rainfall has increased, more rain arrives in heavy downpours, and sea level is rising.

  • Our changing climate is likely to increase damages from floods, reduce crop yields and harm fisheries, increase the number of unpleasantly hot days, and increase the risk of heat stroke and other heat-related illnesses.
