Impacts

  • In the coming decades, Mississippi will become warmer, and both floods and droughts may be more severe.

  • Unlike most of the nation, Mississippi did not become warmer during the last 50 to 100 years.

  • But soils have become drier, annual rainfall has increased, more rain arrives in heavy downpours, and sea level is rising about one inch every seven years.

  • The changing climate is likely to increase damages from tropical storms, reduce crop yields, harm livestock, increase the number of unpleasantly hot days, and increase the risk of heat stroke and other heat-related illnesses.
