Impacts

  • In the coming decades, Oklahoma will become warmer, and both floods and droughts may be more severe.

  • Most of Oklahoma did not become warmer during the last 50 to 100 years.

  • But soils have become drier, annual rainfall has increased, and more rain arrives in heavy downpours.

  • In the coming decades, summers are likely to be increasingly hot and dry, which would reduce the productivity of farms and ranches, change parts of the landscape, and possibly harm human health.
