Impacts

  • Arkansas will become warmer,and the state will probably experience more severe floods and drought.
  • Unlike most of the nation, Arkansas has not become warmer during the last 50 to 100 years.
  • Annual rainfall has increased in much of the state, and more rain arrives in heavy downpours.
  • Changing the climate is likely to increase damage from storms, reduce crop yields, harm livestock,
  • Increase the number of unpleasantly hot days, and increase the risk of heat stroke and other heat-related illnesses.
