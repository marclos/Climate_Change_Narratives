Impacts

  • Indiana’s climate is changing.

  • Most of the state has warmed about one degree (F) in the last century.

  • Floods are becoming more frequent, and ice cover on the Great Lakes is forming later or melting sooner.

  • In the coming decades, the state will have more extremely hot days, which may harm public health in urban areas and corn harvests in rural areas.
