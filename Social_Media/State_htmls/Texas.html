Impacts

  • Contrary to this stations record, most of Texas has warmed between one-half and one degree (F) in the past century.

  • In the eastern two-thirds of the state, average annual rainfall is increasing, yet the soil is becoming drier.

  • Rainstorms are becoming more intense, and floods are becoming more severe.

  • Along much of the coast, the sea is rising almost two inches per decade.

  • In the coming decades, storms are likely to become more severe, deserts may expand, and summers are likely to become increasingly hot and dry, creating problems for agriculture and possibly human health.
